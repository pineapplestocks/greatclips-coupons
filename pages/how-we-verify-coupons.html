How We Verify Great Clips Coupons

We scrape public offer pages, validate the active text, date each coupon, and label the salons the offer actually covers.

Scrape

We scan public Great Clips ad listings and the linked offer pages, then pull the coupon image and visible terms into our dataset.

Validate

We read the visible description and terms, skip hidden boilerplate, and drop anything that clearly says the offer has ended.

Date

New coupons get an added_date, every refresh updates last_seen, and confirmed checks update last_verified.

Mark salons

We tag each offer as US-wide, area-based, or location-specific so the site shows the participating salons honestly.

Our verification flow

1

Collect public offers

The scraper opens the public Facebook Ad Library search for Great Clips coupon content, finds offer URLs, and then visits the official offers.greatclips.com page for each result.

2

Read the visible coupon copy

We pull the text from the live description and terms sections, not the hidden template copy. If the page says the offer has ended, or the coupon is no longer reachable, it does not make it onto the site.

3

Classify the coverage

Each coupon is tagged by the language on the offer page:

US

Valid at participating US salons. These are the broadest offers and usually show without a specific salon name.

AREA

Valid in a named city, metro, or region. We normalize the area into market and keep the exact wording in participating_location_note.

LOCATION

Valid at one salon or address. We keep the salon name, city, and state so the coupon appears only where it should.

UNKNOWN

If we cannot classify an offer confidently, we keep it out of the featured surfaces until it is reviewed or re-verified.

4

Date the offer

We store the scrape snapshot in scraped_at, stamp new items with added_date, update last_seen on repeat checks, and use the coupon's own expiration text when the offer provides one.

5

Keep the labels honest

The site never invents a salon list or rewrites the source wording. If the offer says participating locations, we preserve that language. If the offer is manual, we keep the manual record protected so the automation does not overwrite it.

What we keep in the record

  • valid_text: the exact visible coupon wording from the offer page.
  • expiration: the date printed in the source text when one is present.
  • last_verified: the last successful check against the live offer page.
  • market: a normalized label used to describe US-wide, regional, or location-specific coverage.
  • participating_location_note: a short note that preserves the offer's own "participating" language.
  • manual_add: a flag for protected hand-added records that should not be overwritten by the scraper.

What we do not do

  • We do not invent new promo codes or change the price shown in the source.
  • We do not leave ended offers in the active coupon list.
  • We do not label a salon as participating unless the offer text supports it.
  • We do not hide the source wording that tells you where the coupon works.
